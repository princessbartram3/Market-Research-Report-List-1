<p><p>The pervaporation membranes market is primarily divided into two types: organic and inorganic membranes. Organic pervaporation membranes are typically made from polymeric materials, which offer flexibility and resistance to fouling, making them suitable for separating volatile organic compounds from aqueous solutions. In contrast, inorganic pervaporation membranes, often composed of materials like ceramics or zeolites, provide higher thermal stability and chemical resistance, enabling efficient separation in harsh environments. Both types are essential for applications like wastewater treatment and solvent recovery.</p></p>
