<p><p>The spectrophotometer market is primarily divided into UV-Visible and IR spectrophotometers. UV-Visible spectrophotometers are used to measure light absorption in the ultraviolet to visible spectrum, making them essential in chemistry and biochemistry for analyzing solutions and concentrations. In contrast, IR spectrophotometers operate in the infrared spectrum, allowing for the identification of molecular bonds and functional groups, often used in material science and pharmaceuticals. Both types serve critical roles across research, environmental monitoring, and quality control industries.</p></p>
